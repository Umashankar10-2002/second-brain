# Truthful YouTube Packaging

## Thesis

Good YouTube packaging is not the same thing as misleading clickbait. The useful distinction is not `literal` versus `clickbait`; it is `weak literal description` versus `truthful promise`.

A title can be accurate and still fail because it describes the topic without giving the viewer a felt reason to click. Strong packaging translates the real value of the video into the viewer's language without promising something the video does not deliver.

## Core Distinction

| Mode | Description | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| Literal title | Names the topic plainly. | Often undersells the viewer's reason to care. |
| Clickbait title | Overpromises or distorts the video. | May get clicks but damages trust and taste. |
| Truthful promise | States the strongest honest reason to watch. | Requires more thinking but preserves integrity. |

## Examples From The Wiki

- [[wiki/1,000,000 Villager MANHUNT]] works because the premise is instantly legible: beat Minecraft while one million villagers hunt the player.
- [[wiki/I Must Win MrBeast's $10,000 Refrigerator]] works because it turns a ridiculous prize into a clear stakes engine.
- [[wiki/If MrBeast did a Minecraft Speedrun]] works because the title compresses a parody premise into one sentence.

These examples are not just "clickable." They are clear promises.

## Practical Rule

For each video idea, write three versions:

1. Literal title: the plain accurate version.
2. Strong honest title: the clearest compelling promise.
3. Too-far title: the clickbait version that overpromises.

Publish from version 2.

## Creator Strategy

The creator should separate greenlighting from packaging:

- Greenlight asks: "Is this worth making for the audience, the channel, or my own taste?"
- Packaging asks: "Can I express the strongest honest promise of this video?"

If every idea must look like a hit before it gets made, the creator will slowly stop making anything except high-confidence bets. That protects short-term metrics but can damage creative range.
