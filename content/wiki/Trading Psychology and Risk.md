# Trading Psychology and Risk

## Thesis

The retail trading examples in this vault are less useful as strategy references than as behavioral-risk references. The recurring pattern is not simply that traders are wrong; it is that emotional pressure makes position sizing, time horizon, and identity collapse into one decision.

## Patterns

- Concentrated bets make one outcome feel existential.
- Borrowed money turns a trade into a recovery fantasy.
- Zero-day or short-dated options compress decision time and increase emotional volatility.
- Revenge trading converts a loss into a personal challenge.
- Live trading adds performative pressure, which can encourage rapid bias flips and oversized decisions.

## Source Signals

- [[wiki/The DUMBEST WallStreetBets Trades of October]] shows speculation escalating through loans, risky options, and attempts to recover losses.
- [[wiki/Top 5 WILDEST Live Trading Moments With Reactions]] shows live trading as emotional performance: fast flips, chasing, and public pressure.

## Practical Use

When evaluating trading content, separate:

- strategy: the stated edge or method
- risk design: position sizing, loss limits, time horizon
- psychology: what the trader is likely to do under stress

The psychology layer can dominate the other two.
